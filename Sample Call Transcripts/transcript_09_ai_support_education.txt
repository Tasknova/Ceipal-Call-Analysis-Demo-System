CEIPAL SALES CALL TRANSCRIPT
Project: AI Customer Support Assistant
Duration: ~4 minutes
Participants: James Wilson (Ceipal Sales Agent), Monica Patel (Potential Client - EduLearn Online)

---

James: Good morning, Monica! This is James Wilson from Ceipal. Thanks for making time to chat.

Monica: Good morning, James. I'm interested to hear about your AI solution. We're desperate for help with student support.

James: I'm glad we connected! Let me give you some quick context on Ceipal. We're a 500-person company founded in 2015, headquartered in New Jersey. While we're known for our talent platform, we've built incredible AI solutions across various industries. Our mission is to transform operations with intelligent automation, and education is a sector we're passionate about. Our core values include innovation and customer success - we truly partner with our clients.

Monica: That's good to know. Education definitely needs more innovation.

James: Absolutely. Tell me about your current situation with student support at EduLearn.

Monica: We have about 20,000 active students taking online courses. They have questions 24/7 about course access, assignments, technical issues, payment, certificates. Our support team of 12 can only cover business hours. Nights and weekends, students are stuck waiting for responses, and they get frustrated.

James: That's a huge pain point for online education. Students in different time zones need help when they're studying, not just 9-5. Our AI Customer Support Assistant solves exactly this problem.

Monica: We looked at chatbots before, but they were terrible. Students would ask about assignments and get generic responses that didn't help.

James: Those early chatbots couldn't understand context. Our solution is built on GPT-4 with custom training on your specific courses, curriculum, policies, LMS platform. It actually understands educational terminology and student needs.

Monica: Can it integrate with our LMS? We use Canvas. Students need to access their grades, see which assignments are missing, check deadlines.

James: Perfect - we have direct integration with Canvas, Moodle, Blackboard, all major LMS platforms. When a student asks "What assignments do I have due this week?", the AI authenticates them, pulls their specific course data from Canvas, and lists their upcoming deadlines with links.

Monica: That would be incredibly helpful. What about technical issues? "I can't access the video lectures" or "The quiz isn't loading."

James: The AI can troubleshoot common technical issues - clearing cache, checking browser compatibility, verifying login status. For complex technical problems, it escalates to your tech support team with diagnostic information already collected. Your team doesn't waste time on basic troubleshooting questions.

Monica: What about course-specific questions? If a student asks about a specific concept from their biology course?

James: This is where it gets powerful. We can train the AI on your course content - syllabi, lecture transcripts, textbook materials. It can answer conceptual questions, explain topics, point students to relevant course materials. It's like having a 24/7 teaching assistant for every course.

Monica: Wait, can it actually help with learning? Not just logistics?

James: Absolutely. It can explain concepts in different ways if a student doesn't understand, suggest additional resources, provide examples. It uses Socratic questioning to help students think through problems rather than just giving answers. Many education clients use it as a learning support tool, not just customer service.

Monica: That's much more powerful than I expected. What about privacy and FERPA compliance?

James: Critical for education. The system is designed with FERPA compliance in mind. All student data is encrypted, access is authenticated, full audit logging of every interaction. We can work with your compliance officer to ensure everything meets your requirements.

Monica: Good. We take student privacy very seriously. What kind of volume can it handle?

James: With 20,000 students, you're probably getting 800-1,000 support inquiries daily. The AI can handle unlimited simultaneous conversations. Students get instant responses 24/7, no wait times. For queries it can resolve - probably 65-70% - students get immediate help. Complex issues get escalated to your human team with full context.

Monica: 65-70% resolution would transform our operation. Our team could focus on complex student issues instead of answering "How do I reset my password?" 50 times a day.

James: Exactly. Plus, your human team can provide better support because they're not burned out from repetitive questions. Higher job satisfaction, better student interactions.

Monica: What about implementation timeline? We'd want this ready for fall semester if possible.

James: We can definitely hit that. Implementation for education typically takes 8-10 weeks. First 2-3 weeks is integration with Canvas and training on your course content. Weeks 4-6 are testing with a small group of students. Weeks 7-8 are soft launch across one or two courses. By weeks 9-10, full deployment. If we start within the next month, you'd be ready by late summer for fall semester.

Monica: That timing would be perfect. What about cost?

James: For an online education platform your size - 20,000 students, 800-1,000 daily inquiries - investment runs $85,000-$115,000 for full implementation. That includes LMS integration, course content training, FERPA compliance setup, testing, and go-live support.

Monica: And ongoing costs?

James: Monthly licensing runs $3,500-$4,500 based on student population and interaction volume. That includes hosting, continuous learning from new interactions, updates, and technical support. Now let's talk ROI - if the AI handles 650 inquiries daily, that's equivalent to about 3-4 full-time support staff at $40,000 each. You save $120,000-$160,000 annually.

Monica: So it pays for itself within the first year?

James: Within 8-10 months typically. Plus improved student satisfaction - students get help when they need it, not just during business hours. Better retention, better learning outcomes, better reviews and referrals.

Monica: Do you have case studies from other online education platforms?

James: Absolutely. We recently worked with an online certification program with 15,000 students. They reduced average response time from 4 hours to instant for tier-1 questions, improved student satisfaction scores by 38%, and their support team reported much lower stress levels because they weren't overwhelmed with basic questions.

Monica: That's exactly what we need. What's the next step?

James: I'd love to set up a demo where we show you the AI in action answering student questions, accessing Canvas data, handling course-specific queries. Would you be open to bringing your director of student services and maybe someone from IT?

Monica: Yes, that makes sense. Let me coordinate with them. How about next Wednesday afternoon?

James: Wednesday afternoon works great. I'll send you a calendar invite, that case study I mentioned, technical documentation including FERPA compliance details, and examples of how the AI handles different types of student questions.

Monica: Perfect. Looking forward to it, James.

James: Thank you, Monica. Talk to you Wednesday!
